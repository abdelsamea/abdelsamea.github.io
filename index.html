Biography

I'm a Senior Lecturer in Data and Information Science at the School of Computing and Digital Technology, Birmingham City University. I am also the leading member of the Computer Vision Lab within the DAAI research group at Birmingham City University. I've been working on developing artificial intelligence tools for healthcare and life science applications since 2005. I've been working within several groups of biologists, pathologists, and computer scientists in different research projects in Egypt, Singapore, Italy, and the UK. Before joining Birmingham City University, I worked for the School of Computer Science at Nottingham University, Mechanochemical Cell Biology at Warwick University, Nottingham Molecular Pathology Node (NMPN), and Division of Cancer and Stem Cells both at Nottingham Medical School, as a Research Fellow. In 2016, I got the prestigious Marie Cuire Research Fellowship in the school of Computer Science at Nottingham University. My main research interests are in computer vision including image processing, deep learning, data mining and machine learning, pattern recognition, and biomedical image analysis. I am most interested in developing Artificial Intelligence solutions for healthcare and life science applications. More precisely, my current research interests are concerned with the development of statistical/classical machine learning and deep learning approaches in the areas of medical image segmentation, medical image classification, semantic/instance segmentation, feature extraction, and data mining and machine learning, with the overall ambition to assist human diagnosis. .
